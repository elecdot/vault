## This Week

This week shifted from finishing exams into rebuilding the next set of project work. I closed the remaining 中国经济史, 兵法与商战, and 形势与政策 final exams, then started clarifying the two main course/project tracks: [[statistic-modeling]] and [[hardware-design]]. The middle of the week was uneven and partly paused, but by the end I had a clearer picture of SRDI policy-data work, the labeling problem, `.xlsx` inspection, and the [[pynq]] direction for hardware design.

- Finished several course/exam deliverables: 中国经济史, 兵法与商战, and 形势与政策.
- Clarified the near-term shape of [[statistic-modeling]]: scrape policy data, expand labeled examples, and avoid collecting only "junk" policy samples.
- Confirmed that [[hardware-design]] IP design is manageable, but still needs weekly reporting and steady follow-through.
- Prepared [[projects/statistic-modeling/workspace]] and learned more about GitHub template workflow.
- Learned how to inspect and analyze `.xlsx` files for the data-work side.
- Read multiple SRDI policy references and got a first overview of [[pynq]]: Linux, IPython kernel, the `pynq` package, overlay APIs, and Jupyter as the frontend layer.

## Takeaways

- [[statistic-modeling]] is now less abstract: the next bottleneck is data quality and labeling, not just "starting the project."
- [[hardware-design]] feels less difficult than expected, but the weekly-report requirement makes consistency more important than last-minute effort.
- When energy drops, diary catch-up alone is not enough. I need at least one small artifact per day: a reference note, data sample, project commit, or technical summary.
- The [[pynq]] overview helped connect hardware-design with the actual software/runtime stack, so writing that note should be useful instead of just archival.
