# Naming Convention

## Why
Know how to tell somebody else which naming convention you are using.

## Excerpt
> [!quote] ChatGPT reply
> The naming style `small-case-with-or_as_connector` is generally called **snake_case** in programming and technical writing.
> 
> More precisely:
> 
> * `small_case_with_or_as_connector`
>   → lowercase words separated by underscores (`_`)
>   → **snake_case**
> 
> Related naming conventions:
> 
> | Style                | Example                           |
> | -------------------- | --------------------------------- |
> | snake_case           | `small_case_with_or_as_connector` |
> | camelCase            | `smallCaseWithOrAsConnector`      |
> | PascalCase           | `SmallCaseWithOrAsConnector`      |
> | kebab-case           | `small-case-with-or-as-connector` |
> | SCREAMING_SNAKE_CASE | `SMALL_CASE_WITH_OR_AS_CONNECTOR` |
> 
> Your example mixes:
> 
> * underscores (`_`)
> * and the word `"or"` inside the identifier
> 
> …but the structural convention itself is still snake_case.
> 
> One subtle correction:
> You wrote:
> 
> > "What's the noun of this style of name principle"
> 
> A more natural phrasing would be:
> 
> * “What’s the name of this naming style?”
> * “What is this naming convention called?”
> * “What’s the term for this identifier style?”
> 
> In software engineering, the common umbrella term is:
> 
> * **naming convention**
> * **identifier style**
> * **case style**
> 
> So you could say:
> 
> > "`small_case_with_or_as_connector` uses snake_case naming convention."
